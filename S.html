How to Convert Forms in Word to PDF

By Daniel Hatter

Related

  • How to Convert a PowerPoint to an RTF
  • What Tax Form Does an S Corp Fill Out?
  • How to Wrap Text in Microsoft Excel 2003

Though Microsoft Word is very useful as a document and form authoring program, Word files aren't designed for use as a publishing format. This is mainly due to the fact that the documents are editable and that they aren't viewable online without first being downloaded. Microsoft Word does, however, include a feature that will convert Word documents and forms to PDF; a format specifically designed for publishing and Web use. To convert your business Word forms to PDF, all you need is Microsoft Word.

1

Open the Microsoft Word document containing the form you want to save as a PDF.

2

Open the "File" menu and click "Save As."

3

Select "PDF" from the "Save as type" drop-down menu and choose a PDF formatting option next to "Optimize for." Choose "Standard" to save it as a regular-size PDF file or choose "Minimum size" if you plan to publish it online.

4

Use the dialog box to navigate to the desired folder and click the "Save" button to save the form as a PDF file.
